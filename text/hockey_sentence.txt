gliding forward, The player is moving forward across the ice. His skates are pointed ahead and he is not pushing off with his legs. His body is slightly leaned forward and his knees are bent. His arms are relaxed at his sides as he smoothly glides.
accelerating forward, The player is skating forward and increasing his speed. He is pushing off the ice with strong strides from his legs. His body is leaning forward and his arms are moving in rhythm with his strides. His skates dig into the ice as he gains momentum.
gliding backward, The player is moving backward on the ice. His skates are angled outward and he is not taking active strides. His knees are bent and his upper body stays balanced over his hips. He continues to coast smoothly in a backward direction.
accelerating backward, The player is skating backward and picking up speed. He pushes outward with alternating legs to generate force. His knees stay bent and his torso remains steady. His arms help maintain balance as he moves faster in reverse.
forward-to-backward transition, The player is changing from forward skating to backward skating. He rotates his hips and shoulders while turning his skates. One foot pivots on the ice as he shifts direction. By the end of the movement, he is facing the opposite way and moving backward.
backward-to-forward transition, The player is switching from skating backward to skating forward. He twists his upper body and pivots his skates to turn around. His weight shifts from one edge of the skate to the other. He finishes the motion facing forward and continues moving ahead.
post-whistle gliding, The player is slowly gliding on the ice after play has stopped. His posture is more upright and relaxed. He is not skating with effort and makes no sudden movements. He drifts calmly as he waits for the next play to begin.
faceoff body position, The player is crouched low on the ice, preparing for a faceoff. His knees are bent deeply and his stick is placed on the ice in front of him. His upper body leans forward and his eyes focus on the puck drop area. He remains still, ready to react.
maintain position, The player is staying in one area on the ice. He makes small adjustments with short movements of his skates. His posture stays balanced and alert. He is watching the play while holding his position.
prone on ice, The player is lying flat on the ice surface. His body is stretched out and close to the ice. His arms and legs may be extended or tucked slightly. He is not in an upright skating position.
on one knee, The player is down on one knee on the ice. One skate remains on the ice while the other knee touches the surface. His upper body is slightly leaned forward for balance. He may be getting up or stabilizing himself.
