gliding forward, head faces forward; hand stays relaxed at sides; arm swings slightly with motion; hip leans slightly forward; leg extends and glides without active push; foot stays in contact with the ice and points forward.
accelerating forward, head faces forward; hand swings in rhythm with strides; arm pumps forward and back; hip leans forward to drive motion; leg pushes forcefully against the ice in alternating strides; foot digs into the ice and pushes backward.
gliding backward, head faces forward or slightly over shoulder; hand stays relaxed near sides; arm held slightly out for balance; hip stays centered over skates; leg glides without strong push; foot stays on the ice with toes angled outward.
accelerating backward, head looks forward over shoulder; hand moves slightly for balance; arm swings gently with skating motion; hip stays low and stable; leg pushes outward in alternating backward strides; foot presses into the ice to generate backward force.
forward-to-backward transition, head turns in the direction of the turn; hand shifts slightly for balance; arm rotates with upper body; hip rotates to change skating direction; leg pivots on the ice during the turn; foot rolls from one edge to the other to complete the transition.
backward-to-forward transition, head turns toward new direction of travel; hand adjusts outward for balance; arm rotates with torso; hip twists to reorient the body forward; leg pivots while shifting weight; foot changes edge to guide the turn into forward motion.
post-whistle gliding, head looks around the ice; hand rests loosely by sides; arm relaxed with little movement; hip upright and relaxed; leg glides slowly without strong strides; foot remains on the ice with minimal edge pressure.
faceoff body position, head angled downward toward the puck area; hand grips the stick firmly; arm bends at the elbow holding the stick on the ice; hip lowered into a crouched stance; leg bent deeply at the knees; foot planted firmly on the ice for stability.
maintain position, head tracks the play; hand remains near the stick; arm held ready but mostly still; hip stays centered over skates; leg makes small adjustments without full strides; foot shifts slightly on the ice to hold position.
prone on ice, head turned to one side or facing down; hand placed on the ice or tucked near body; arm bent close to the torso; hip resting on the ice; leg extended or slightly bent; foot lying flat against the ice surface.
on one knee, head facing forward or downward; hand may rest on stick or ice for balance; arm bent to support posture; hip lowered toward the kneeling leg; leg bent with one knee on the ice and the other skate upright; foot of the standing leg flat on the ice for support.
